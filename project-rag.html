An end-to-end question-answering system over SQuAD 2.0 that grounds its answers in retrieved evidence, combining dense retrieval, cross-encoder re-ranking, and a LoRA-fine-tuned reader.

RoleResearcher & Developer

The Problem

A language model asked a factual question will answer confidently whether or not it actually knows. Retrieval-augmented generation tackles that by grounding the answer in evidence: instead of relying on what the model has memorised, the system first retrieves relevant passages and answers from them. SQuAD 2.0 sharpens the challenge further, because a third of its questions have no answer in the text at all, so the system has to know when to abstain rather than invent something.

What I Built

A full pipeline from a raw passage corpus to a grounded answer: encode and index the passages, retrieve candidates for a question, re-rank them for precision, and read the answer from the best context, fine-tuning the reader efficiently with LoRA. The goal was not a single model but a working system where each stage earns its place.

The interesting part of grounding a model isn't teaching it to answer. It's teaching it to know when it can't.

Why It Mattered

Beyond the marks, the project was a way to work hands-on with the parts of modern NLP I care most about: how retrieval and generation fit together, where a pipeline's quality is actually won or lost, and how to adapt a large model on a student's compute budget without retraining it from scratch.

The Pipeline

Four stages, each addressing a different failure mode. Retrieval finds candidate evidence fast; re-ranking sharpens its precision; the reader extracts the answer; LoRA makes adapting that reader affordable.

Dense indexing
The passage corpus is encoded into dense vector embeddings and stored in a FAISS index, which supports fast approximate nearest-neighbour search over the whole collection. This is what makes retrieval scale beyond a brute-force comparison.
FAISS
2
Dense retrieval
A question is embedded into the same vector space, and FAISS returns its top-k nearest passages by semantic similarity, not keyword overlap, so paraphrased or indirectly worded questions still surface the right context.
Top-k
3
Cross-encoder re-ranking
Dense retrieval is fast but coarse. A cross-encoder then scores each question-and-passage pair jointly, reading them together rather than comparing two separate embeddings, and re-orders the candidates. This precision step is where much of the answer quality is won.
Precision
4
Reading with a LoRA-tuned reader
A transformer reader extracts the answer span from the top-ranked passage, or abstains when SQuAD 2.0 offers no answer. The reader is adapted with LoRA: small low-rank adapters are trained while the base weights stay frozen, giving most of the benefit of full fine-tuning at a fraction of the cost.
LoRA

Why LoRA

Full fine-tuning of a large reader is expensive and memory-hungry. LoRA, low-rank adaptation, freezes the original weights and learns a small set of additional parameters instead. The result is a reader tuned to the task that trains quickly and runs on modest hardware, which mattered for a project built on a student's resources rather than a research cluster.

Results

On SQuAD 2.0 the system reached an F1 of 66.4 and an exact match of 62.8. The gap between the two is itself informative: F1 gives partial credit for answers that overlap the gold span, while exact match demands the answer be word-for-word correct, so the spread reflects answers that are nearly but not perfectly aligned.

Where Each Choice Earned Its Place

The pipeline was built so that every component addressed a specific weakness of the one before it.

Stage Choice Why it mattered
Retrieval Dense embeddings + FAISS Fast semantic search over the corpus; finds evidence that keyword matching would miss
Re-ranking Cross-encoder Joint scoring of question and passage lifts the truly relevant context to the top, improving what the reader sees
Reading Transformer + LoRA Extracts the answer span; LoRA adapts the reader efficiently without full fine-tuning
Abstention No-answer handling SQuAD 2.0 rewards knowing when not to answer; getting this right protects the score on unanswerable questions

What I'd Explore Next

The clearest levers from here are the retrieval and re-ranking depth, how many candidates to pull and how aggressively to re-score them, and the abstention threshold, which trades answering more questions against answering wrong ones. Both are the kind of tuning where a pipeline's real-world reliability is decided, well after the headline architecture is in place.
