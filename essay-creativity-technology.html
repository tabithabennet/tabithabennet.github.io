Creativity Is a Technology - We Just Don't Treat It Like One

Every framework, method, and tool we use to generate ideas is a technology. Recognising that changes how we develop, share, and scale creative thinking.

TopicAI & Creativity
Read time6 min read
ByTabitha Bennet

The Category Error

We have a strange habit of treating creativity as the opposite of technology. Technology is systematic, reproducible, scalable. Creativity is mysterious, personal, unreliable. One is engineering. The other is art. Never the twain.

This is a category error, and it's costing us enormously.

What Technology Actually Is

Technology, in its broadest sense, is any systematic method for achieving a goal. Writing is a technology. Double-entry bookkeeping is a technology. The scientific method is a technology. They are all systems that, when learned and applied, reliably produce outcomes that would otherwise be difficult or impossible to achieve.

By this definition, creative practice is absolutely a technology. Brainstorming protocols, constraint-based ideation, analogical thinking, lateral problem-solving - these are methods with documented, reproducible effects on creative output. They can be taught, practised, refined, and scaled.

When we mystify creativity, we make it inaccessible. When we treat it as a technology, we make it teachable.

The AI Disruption

Generative AI has made this conversation urgent in a new way. Large language models and image generators can now produce outputs that were, until very recently, exclusively the domain of trained human creatives. This has triggered enormous anxiety in creative industries.

But I think the anxiety is misdirected. What AI can replicate is the surface of creative output - the stylistic patterns, the compositional conventions, the genre markers. What it cannot replicate is creative judgement: the ability to decide what is worth making, what problem actually needs solving, what response a specific human audience in a specific moment needs to receive.

The New Creative Stack

I've restructured how I work over the past two years to reflect this. AI handles the generative volume - the first drafts, the variations, the structural options. I handle the selection, refinement, and contextual judgement. The output is better than either of us alone.

This isn't the death of creativity. It's the emergence of a new creative stack - one where the humans who understand both the tools and the judgement layer will produce work that neither could achieve independently.

Creativity is a technology. AI is making it a faster one. Our job is to make sure we're still the ones deciding what it's for.
